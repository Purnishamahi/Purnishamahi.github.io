Peony

Symbolism

A symbol of timeless romance, whispered wishes, and quiet grace.

Scientific Name

Paeonia — as delicate in sound as it is in bloom.

In the Language of Flowers

The peony is a bloom that speaks in blushing tones. To lovers, it murmurs of admiration left unspoken; to poets, it offers petals full of longing.

In every era, this flower has danced between elegance and emotion — a muse in gardens and bouquets alike.

Cultural Roots

  • China: Revered as the "King of Flowers" — a mark of honor, elegance, and prosperity, found in palaces and poetry alike.
  • Japan: A talisman of good fortune and a happy marriage, gracing family crests and springtime festivals.
  • Victorian England: A secret signature of bashful affection, passed quietly in love letters and hand-tied nosegays.

History

With roots deep in ancient soil, the story of the peony winds through empires and myths.

In Chinese dynasties, it bloomed as a royal treasure.

In Greek legend, it was born from divine transformation — Paeon, healer of gods, turned into this bloom to be saved from envy.

Through centuries, its petals have symbolized healing, magic, and heartful emotion. Wherever it grows, the peony brings with it a soft magic — one that lingers like perfume in the air.
