Rose

Symbolism

A symbol of love, passion, beauty, and secrecy — each petal a whisper of emotion.

  • Red: Romantic love, passion
  • White: Purity, innocence, reverence
  • Pink: Gratitude, admiration, sweetness
  • Yellow: Friendship, joy, jealousy (Victorian)
  • Orange: Desire, enthusiasm

Scientific Name

Rosa

In the Language of Flowers

The rose is the most iconic bloom in floriography — a timeless messenger of love. In the Victorian era, a single red rose could declare deep affection, while a pink rose expressed tender admiration. Bouquets often blended colors and numbers to craft secret messages only the receiver could decode.

Cultural Roots

  • Ancient Greece & Rome: Sacred to Aphrodite and Venus, the goddesses of love.
  • Persia: A poetic emblem of the soul and divine beauty.
  • Christianity: Associated with the Virgin Mary as a symbol of purity.
  • Islamic Gardens: Signifier of paradise and mystical beauty.
  • Medieval Europe: Used in heraldry, most famously in the War of the Roses.

History

Roses have been cultivated for over 5,000 years, first in China and the Middle East. The flower became a symbol of indulgence in Roman feasts and a treasured icon in Islamic and European gardens.

During England’s War of the Roses, the red and white blooms represented two rival houses. In the 19th century, the Victorians perfected the rose’s role as a wordless expression of the heart.
