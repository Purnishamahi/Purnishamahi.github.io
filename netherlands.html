Netherlands Flower Festivals

Keukenhof Gardens

Often referred to as the "Garden of Europe," Keukenhof is one of the most famous and expansive flower gardens in the world. Spanning over 79 acres in the town of Lisse, it features more than 7 million flower bulbs each spring. The gardens are designed around annually changing themes and include pavilions, water features, and art installations. Open from mid-March to mid-May, it is a celebration of Dutch horticulture and a global symbol of tulip beauty.

Bloemencorso Bollenstreek

This vibrant flower parade stretches from Noordwijk to Haarlem, covering a 42-kilometer route lined with thousands of spectators. Each year in April, elaborately decorated floats, cars, and performers make their way through towns, adorned with tulips, hyacinths, and daffodils. The event celebrates the blooming of the bulb-growing region and is an iconic tradition blending floral artistry, community spirit, and festive energy.

Varend Corso Westland

Held in the summer, the Varend Corso Westland is a floating flower festival unique to the Netherlands. Boats cruise through canals decorated with a dazzling array of flowers, fruits, and vegetables. This waterborne spectacle is accompanied by live music, costumed performers, and cheering spectators along the shores. It's a fresh and festive way of showcasing Dutch floriculture and the region's strong greenhouse culture.

Bloemencorso Zundert

Bloemencorso Zundert, held in early September, is the largest flower parade in the world built entirely with dahlias. Created by 20 competing hamlets, the floats can reach up to 9 meters high and are crafted with incredible detail and creativity. The event started in 1936 and has since become a beloved community effort and international attraction. Each float is a moving work of art celebrating imagination, teamwork, and floral mastery.
