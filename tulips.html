Tulip

Symbolism

Graceful, sincere, and full of promise — the tulip blooms with gentle affection.

  • Red Tulip: True love and passion
  • Yellow Tulip: Cheerfulness and sunshine
  • Purple Tulip: Royalty and elegance
  • White Tulip: Forgiveness and purity

Scientific Name

Tulipa (from the Persian word *delband*, meaning turban — for its shape)

In the Language of Flowers

Tulips carry messages of quiet love and refined emotion. They’re a way of saying “I care,” often without drama or demand. Their graceful shape and vibrant petals make them ideal for heartfelt yet elegant expressions.

Cultural Roots

  • Turkey & Persia: First cultivated; symbol of paradise on Earth. A sacred flower in Ottoman art and textiles.
  • Netherlands: Iconic in Dutch history — became a national symbol and centerpiece of “Tulip Mania.”
  • Victorian Era: A quiet declaration of love, often color-coded to express subtle intentions.

History

Tulips originated in Central Asia, later blooming in Persian and Turkish gardens. By the 16th century, they spread to Europe and sparked a frenzy — especially in the Netherlands during the 1630s. "Tulip Mania" became the first recorded economic bubble.

Since then, tulips have continued to enchant gardeners, artists, and lovers with their elegant simplicity and deep emotional resonance.
