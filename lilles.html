Lily

Symbolism

Purity, renewal, motherhood, and majesty — the lily glows with sacred grace.

  • White Lily: Innocence and purity
  • Stargazer Lily: Ambition and prosperity
  • Calla Lily: Elegance and rebirth
  • Orange Lily: Confidence and passion

Scientific Name

Lilium

In the Language of Flowers

Lilies carry deep spiritual and emotional messages. A white lily whispers of innocence, often gifted at weddings and funerals alike — symbols of both beginnings and farewells. To offer lilies is to speak of devotion, dignity, and remembrance.

Cultural Roots

  • Ancient Greece: Linked to Hera, goddess of women and marriage.
  • Christianity: Represents the Virgin Mary's purity — often depicted in sacred art.
  • China: A wedding flower, wishing for 100 years of love and harmony.
  • Victorian Era: Symbol of refined beauty and virtue.

History

Traced back to 1580 B.C. in Minoan Crete, lilies have long been revered. In ancient times, their bulbs were used in medicines and rituals. Roman brides wore crowns of lilies to signify purity. As centuries passed, the lily maintained its holy presence, blooming in literature, religion, and regal gardens across the world.
