Japan's Major Flower Festivals

Kawazu Cherry Blossom Festival

Held in the charming town of Kawazu in Shizuoka Prefecture, this festival starts as early as February, making it one of the first cherry blossom events of the year in Japan. Visitors can stroll along an 8km stretch of the Kawazu River, surrounded by thousands of vibrant pink sakura trees.

The blossoms are from a special early-blooming variety called *Kawazu-zakura*, known for their rich color and longevity. At night, the trees are illuminated, creating a dreamy and romantic atmosphere for evening walks.

Fuji Shibazakura Festival

Every spring, the foothills near Lake Motosu transform into a sea of pink, white, and purple moss phlox during the Shibazakura Festival. With Mount Fuji providing a majestic backdrop, the view is nothing short of iconic.

Over 800,000 shibazakura (moss phlox) plants bloom in artistic arrangements across the fields. In addition to breathtaking views, the festival features local food stalls, cultural displays, and foot baths overlooking the blooms.

Kawachi Wisteria Garden

This hidden gem in Kitakyushu, Fukuoka, is world-famous for its mesmerizing wisteria tunnels. The garden opens to the public only during peak bloom in late April and early May, attracting thousands of floral enthusiasts.

The garden features over 20 varieties of wisteria in colors ranging from lavender and purple to white and pale pink. The 80-meter-long tunnel creates a surreal floral canopy that’s often described as stepping into a fairytale.

Nemophila Harmony – Hitachi Seaside Park

Hitachi Seaside Park in Ibaraki Prefecture becomes a dreamlike blue wonderland during late April to early May when 5 million nemophila, or "baby blue eyes", bloom across the vast Miharashi Hill.

The entire hill turns sky blue, mirroring the heavens above and offering panoramic ocean views in the distance. It's one of Japan’s most photogenic and peaceful flower festivals, with plenty of open space for relaxing and soaking in the view.
