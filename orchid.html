Orchid

Symbolism

Luxury, mystery, sensuality, and rare beauty — the orchid is an icon of grace and allure.

  • Purple Orchid: Royalty and admiration
  • White Orchid: Purity and reverence
  • Pink Orchid: Joy and femininity
  • Blue Orchid: Rarity and uniqueness

Scientific Name

Orchidaceae

In the Language of Flowers

Orchids speak in a language of fascination and elegance. To gift one is to say: "You are rare, you are exquisite." In floriography, they express mature charm, refinement, and a touch of the exotic. Ideal for romantic gestures and grand statements alike.

Cultural Roots

  • China: Associated with integrity and refinement — one of the "Four Noble Ones" in traditional art.
  • Japan: A symbol of wealth and bravery, often admired by samurai and nobility.
  • Aztecs: Used in a mixture of vanilla and cacao, believed to give strength and power.
  • Victorian Era: Seen as exotic treasures — rare, coveted, and sensual.

History

With over 25,000 species, orchids are among the most diverse and ancient blooms. First documented in ancient Greece, they were believed to influence virility and strength. In the 1800s, orchid collecting became an obsession among European aristocracy — called "orchidelirium."

Today, they continue to captivate with their delicate forms, exotic beauty, and aura of quiet mystery.
