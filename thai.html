Thailand's Flower Festivals

Chiang Mai Flower Festival

This iconic festival held in early February in northern Thailand is famous for its stunning floral parade, massive floats adorned with orchids and chrysanthemums, and the crowning of the Flower Queen. The entire city blooms with vibrant displays of local horticulture.

Suan Luang Rama IX Flower Show

Held in Bangkok’s largest public park, this floral event in December celebrates King Rama IX’s birthday. Visitors enjoy landscaped gardens, lotus ponds, bonsai displays, and exhibitions of rare Thai and international flowers in beautifully designed spaces.

Lopburi Sunflower Festival

Taking place from November to January, this radiant celebration showcases thousands of acres of golden sunflowers. It’s a photographer’s paradise and a family favorite, where fields stretch as far as the eye can see under clear blue skies.

Dok Krachiao Blossom Festival

Held in Chaiyaphum Province from June to August, this lesser-known festival celebrates the rare and wild beauty of Siam Tulips (Dok Krachiao). Nature lovers flock to Pa Hin Ngam National Park to witness entire meadows covered in these vibrant purple-pink blooms.
